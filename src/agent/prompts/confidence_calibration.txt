Self-review your findings to establish a Statistical Confidence level based on the quality and quantity of evidence gathered.

Confidence levels:
- High: multiple corroborating evidence sources, or 3+ temporal snapshots showing a clear trend.
- Medium: a single explicit, quantifiable evidence source (e.g., a confirmed allocation size in code, or consistent single-snapshot data).
- Low: indirect or inferred evidence only.

Raising/Lowering requests or limits requires at least medium confidence.

With Low confidence, recommend "COMMENT" rather than "REQUEST_CHANGES" and describe what additional evidence is needed.
